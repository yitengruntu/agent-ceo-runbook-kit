# Decision Gates

## Gate 1: Can The Agent Continue Alone?

Continue when:

- The next action is local, reversible, and useful.
- The output can be validated.
- The work creates a sellable asset, measurable surface, or clearer decision.

Stop when:

- External posting is needed.
- Payment, tax, identity, or account setup is needed.
- Real customer/platform data is needed.
- The agent would only rewrite docs without changing the evidence picture.

## Gate 2: Is This Real Demand?

Count as evidence:

- External visits from a real channel.
- Saves, favorites, comments, replies, downloads, installs, or checkout starts.
- Direct questions from people who fit the buyer profile.
- Orders, even at a low price.

Do not count:

- Owner testing.
- Localhost traffic.
- Bot traffic.
- Server IP hostname traffic.
- Free coupon orders used only to test checkout.
- Search impressions for irrelevant queries.

## Gate 3: Continue, Revise, Kill

Continue when:

- The offer creates qualified intent within the planned window.
- The buyer language is clearer after launch.
- The next version can be smaller or more specific.

Revise when:

- People click but do not convert.
- People ask for a related but different solution.
- The channel works but the offer is vague.

Kill when:

- No qualified external intent appears after a complete distribution attempt.
- The channel is too slow for the experiment's purpose.
- The product requires owner actions that cannot be delegated.

## Gate 4: Pivot

Pivot only with a written decision:

- What failed
- What evidence supports the failure
- What reusable asset remains
- What new hypothesis replaces it
